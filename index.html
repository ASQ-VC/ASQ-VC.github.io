ASR-Guided Discrete Representation Learning for High-Fidelity Voice Conversion

Disentangling speech content from speaker-specific characteristics (e.g., timbre, accent) is critical for high-quality voice conversion (VC). Existing methods face a trade-off: narrow bottlenecks remove speaker information but degrade linguistic fidelity, while wide bottlenecks preserve content at the cost of residual timbre leakage. To address this, we propose a novel framework combining ASR-guided discrete content extraction and a VITS-based synthesis model. Our method introduces finite scalar quantization (FSQ) with explicit ASR supervision, forcing the bottleneck to retain linguistic content while discarding speaker traits. The quantized content representations are then integrated into a modified VITS architecture, enhanced with a transformer-based timbre encoder and speaker verification embeddings. Experiments on LibriTTS and VCTK datasets show state-of-the-art performance, achieving 11.2% WER (measured via HuBERT-large ASR) and 0.446 SECS (via WavLM TDNN), surpassing prior SOTA method by absolute value 2.6% and 1.3% respectively. Ablation studies further validate the effectiveness of ASR guidance and hierarchical codebook design. This work provides a robust solution for content-preserving and speaker-consistent voice conversion.
